## 1. Brand Overview

### 1.1 Name
**HaloHelm**

Pronounced *hay-low-helm*. Always one word, capitalised "H" twice (HaloHelm, not Halohelm or Halo Helm).

### 1.2 Etymology + Meaning
- **Halo** — a bright ring of light, divine aura, illumination. Represents *clarity, guidance, premium quality*.
- **Helm** — the wheel that steers a ship; also a knight's helmet. Represents *command, leadership, control* ("at the helm").
- **Combined meaning**: A restaurant being led with both light AND command. A captain at the helm with a halo above. *Restaurant operators in control of a system that elevates them.*

### 1.3 Tagline
> **Your restaurant. At the helm.**

(Also acceptable in spoken/long form: "Run your restaurant at the helm.")

### 1.4 Positioning
- **What it is**: A SaaS platform for Indian restaurants. Customers scan a QR at their table, browse the menu (with AR visualisation of dishes), order, and pay — restaurant staff manage everything from a clean admin dashboard.
- **Who it's for**: Indian restaurant owners + their staff. Tech-comfortable enough to use a smartphone, not necessarily technical.
- **Why it exists**: Restaurants want modern customer experiences without the cost / lock-in of large chains. HaloHelm is the boutique-quality system at a small-restaurant price.
- **What it competes with**: Petpooja, Zomato Pro, paper menus, generic QR-menu apps.
- **Tone of voice**: confident, calm, polished. Not loud, not techy, not cute.

### 1.5 Brand Adjectives (pick any 3, use as creative compass)
- Heraldic
- Premium
- Calm
- Confident
- Refined
- Modern-classical
- Trustworthy
- Quiet luxury

### 1.6 What it is NOT
- ❌ Loud / disruptive ("DELIVERY APP" energy)
- ❌ Cute / playful ("burger emojis")
- ❌ Generic SaaS-tech ("blue gradient + sans-serif")
- ❌ Heavy / cluttered ("medieval coat of arms with 9 elements")
- ❌ Cheap-looking
- ❌ Food-focused imagery (this is software, not food delivery)

## 2. Visual Identity

### 2.1 Colour Palette (Aspire palette)

Use these EXACT hex values. Don't pick "close enough" colours.

| Colour | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Matte Black (Ink)** | `#1A1A1A` | Primary brand colour. Dark backgrounds. Body text on cream. |
| **Cream (Light bg)** | `#EDEDED` | Light backgrounds. App canvas. |
| **Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | Card backgrounds. Reverse text on dark. |
| **Gold (Primary accent)** | `#C4A86D` | Highlights. Accent strokes. The "Helm" word in the wordmark. |
| **Gold Dim** | `#A08656` | Darker gold for text on light backgrounds (contrast). |

**Combinations**:
- **Hero combo**: Matte black background + Gold mark + Cream/white text
- **Light combo**: Cream background + Matte black mark + Gold accents
- **Avoid**: ever using both Cream (#EDEDED) and White (#FFFFFF) in the same composition (they're too similar — pick one)

### 2.2 Typography
**Inter** (primary, only). Already used throughout the app.

- For wordmark: Inter Semibold (600) or Bold (700)
- For "Helm" half of the wordmark: Inter Medium-Italic (500-italic) in Gold (`#C4A86D`)
- For taglines: Inter Regular (400) or Medium (500)
- For UI labels: Inter Semibold (600), letter-spacing slightly increased (`0.08em`)

**The existing wordmark format** (used in admin chrome):
```
Halo[regular ink]Helm[italic gold]
```
e.g. **Halo***Helm* — with "Helm" in italic gold.

## 3. Asset 1 — Logo (B.1)

### 3.1 Concept

> **A clean helmet outline with a halo ring above it.**

Heraldic, but radically simplified. Think: if a 13th-century knight's coat of arms had a graphic-design degree from 2026.

**Composition**:
- The helmet is the dominant element (centred, takes ~60% of the canvas)
- The halo is a thin ring suspended above the helmet (not touching — small gap)
- Both elements in **line art** (1.5-2.5px stroke), not filled
- Geometric, clean — every curve feels intentional
- Symmetrical along the vertical axis

**Helmet style options** (pick one — these are mood references, designer picks the final shape):

| Style | Vibe | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Closed-visor knight's helm** (tournament style, T-shaped slit) | Classic European heraldic | Most traditional / safest pick |
| **Spartan / Greek hoplite helm** (with crest from front to back) | Classical antiquity, "leader" | Strongest "at the helm" reading |
| **Simplified Norman / Templar helm** (conical with nose guard) | Stripped-down medieval | Most minimal / boldest |

**Recommendation**: **Greek hoplite-style helm** with a small horizontal-cut visor and a subtle crest line. Most readable + ties to the "helm = leadership" word play.

### 3.2 Halo treatment

- A **thin ring** (open circle, not a disc) directly above the helmet
- Centred horizontally with the helmet
- ~30-40% the width of the helmet
- ~10-15px gap between halo bottom and helmet top
- Same stroke weight as the helmet outline

**Optional refinement**: a single small dot or break in the ring at the top, as a heraldic accent.

### 3.3 Colour treatment

Three versions of the same mark:

| Version | Helmet | Halo | Background | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Gold `#C4A86D` | Gold `#C4A86D` | Matte black `#1A1A1A` | App icon, splash screen, dark contexts |
| **Inverse** | Matte black `#1A1A1A` | Gold `#C4A86D` | Cream `#EDEDED` or White `#FFFFFF` | Light backgrounds, print, footer |
| **Monochrome** | Single colour (black OR white) | Same | Transparent | Single-colour print, embroidery, favicons |

### 3.4 Deliverables — Logo set

| Asset | Dimensions | Format | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary mark — light bg** | 1024×1024 | PNG (transparent), SVG | Black ink + gold halo on transparent |
| **Primary mark — dark bg** | 1024×1024 | PNG (transparent), SVG | Gold on transparent (renders well over the matte-black bg) |
| **Monochrome — white** | 1024×1024 | PNG (transparent), SVG | All-white for dark backgrounds |
| **Monochrome — black** | 1024×1024 | PNG (transparent), SVG | All-black for light backgrounds |
| **Wordmark horizontal** | 2048×512 (4:1) | PNG (transparent), SVG | Mark on the left + "Halo*Helm*" wordmark on the right. For website headers, email signatures, etc. |
| **Wordmark stacked** | 1024×1280 | PNG (transparent), SVG | Mark on top, wordmark centred below. For square contexts. |

**File naming convention** (so the engineer can wire them in cleanly):

- `logo-primary-dark.svg` / `.png`

## 4. Asset 2 — OG Share Image (B.4)

### 4.1 Purpose

The **Open Graph image** shown when someone shares `halohelm.com` on WhatsApp, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Slack, iMessage, Facebook, etc. It's the "preview card" — first impression for anyone discovering HaloHelm via a shared link.

### 4.2 Dimensions

**1200 × 630 pixels** (1.91:1 ratio). PNG format. Under 1 MB ideal.

### 4.3 Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  ┌──────────┐                                           │
│  │  [LOGO]  │  HaloHelm                                 │
│  └──────────┘                                           │
│                                                         │
│                                                         │
│       Your restaurant. At the helm.                     │
│                                                         │
│       AR-powered menus. Modern POS. Indian restaurants. │
│                                                         │
│                                                         │
│                                          [subtle helm]  │
│                                          [logo BG fade] │
│  halohelm.com                                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Composition rules**:
- **Background**: Matte black (`#1A1A1A`) with a subtle radial gradient from one corner (gold at ~8% opacity, fading to nothing). Adds depth without distraction.
- **Logo + wordmark**: top-left corner, with ~60px padding from edges. Logo ~80px square, wordmark Inter Bold 36px next to it.
- **Headline**: Centred vertically + horizontally. Inter Bold 64px, cream `#EDEDED`. The tagline: **Your restaurant. At the helm.**
- **Sub-headline**: Below headline. Inter Regular 26px, gold-dim `#A08656`. Sub-description text (one line).
- **Watermark**: A very faint, large version of the helm logo in the bottom-right corner, ~25% opacity, gold colour. Adds visual richness without clutter.
- **URL footer**: bottom-left, `halohelm.com` in Inter Medium 22px, cream at 60% opacity.

### 4.4 Don'ts (Critical for OG images)

- ❌ Don't crowd the text — OG cards are often shown at 300px wide in social feeds; tiny details disappear.
- ❌ Don't use photos of food (this isn't a food brand).
- ❌ Don't include screenshots of the app (too detailed at preview size).
- ❌ Don't use a busy background pattern.
- ❌ Don't put critical content in the outer 5% (some platforms crop slightly).

### 4.5 Deliverables — OG image

| Asset | Dimensions | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **OG image (default)** | 1200×630 | PNG, < 1 MB |
| **OG image (square)** | 1080×1080 | PNG, < 1 MB | *(optional — for Instagram / WhatsApp story sharing)* |
| **Twitter card (large)** | 1200×675 | PNG, < 1 MB | *(optional — slightly different ratio)* |

**File naming**:
- `og-default.png` (1200×630)
- `og-square.png` (1080×1080)
- `og-twitter.png` (1200×675)

Place in `/public/` directory of the codebase.

## 6. How to Pick a Designer (if going human route)

### 6.1 Where to find designers
- **Fiverr** — quick, cheap (₹500-2,000 per asset). Filter for "logo design India" + "vector logo".
- **Upwork** — mid-tier (₹3,000-15,000 for full brand identity). Look for ratings 4.9+ with 50+ jobs.
- **99designs** — design contests (₹15,000-50,000). 30-50 designers submit, you pick a winner.
- **Behance / Dribbble** — high-end portfolios. Reach out to designers whose work matches the heraldic-modern aesthetic. ₹30,000-1,00,000+ for a complete identity.
- **Local Chennai / Puducherry studios** — search "brand design studio Chennai".

### 6.2 What to send them

1. **This entire document**.
2. A note saying: *"Above is the full brief. Please review Section 1 (Brand) and Section 2 (Visual Identity) first to absorb the tone. Then deliver everything in Section 3.4, Section 3.5, and Section 4.5."*
3. Your budget range and deadline.

### 6.3 Red flags when reviewing designers
- ❌ Their portfolio is full of busy, colourful designs (wrong aesthetic for HaloHelm)
- ❌ They suggest using stock fonts / emoji-based icons
- ❌ They want to use Comic Sans, Papyrus, or anything calligraphic
- ❌ They don't ask follow-up questions (they're not engaging with the brand)
- ❌ They say "I can do it in 1 hour" for less than ₹500 (you'll get a generic stock-asset edit, not a real design)

### 6.4 Cost expectations (in INR)

| Scope | Realistic budget |
|---|---|
| Logo only (single primary mark) | ₹3,000 - 10,000 |
| Logo + favicon set | ₹5,000 - 15,000 |
| Logo + favicons + OG image | ₹8,000 - 25,000 |
| Full brand identity (logo, favicons, OG, business cards, letterhead, etc.) | ₹25,000 - 1,00,000 |

For HaloHelm at this stage, **₹5,000-15,000 for logo + favicons + OG** is reasonable. Negotiate based on portfolio quality, not price alone.
